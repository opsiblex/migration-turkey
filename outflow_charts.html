Where Do Turkish Citizens Go?

First residence permits issued to Turkish citizens by EU/EEA countries, broken down by reason. The data reveals a significant rise in outward migration — particularly for employment and family reasons — with Germany remaining the dominant destination throughout the observed period.

+117%
Rise in total permits
From 60,000 permits in 2015 to 130,000 in 2023 — a more than doubling of Turkish citizens receiving first residence permits in the EU, driven by economic instability and political uncertainty in Turkey.
+685%
Employment permits surge
Employment-based permits grew from 5,100 in 2015 to 40,100 in 2023 — a nearly sevenfold increase. The sharpest rise occurred between 2021 and 2022, coinciding with Turkey's currency crisis and high inflation.
#1
Family — largest single category
Family reunification consistently accounts for the largest share of permits — around 35% across all years. Germany alone issued 18,770 family permits to Turkish citizens in 2024, reflecting decades of established diaspora.
🇩🇪 34%
Germany dominates
Germany receives approximately one third of all Turkish outflow permits every year. Poland has emerged as the second largest destination since 2021, particularly for employment, reflecting new migration corridors in Eastern Europe.
